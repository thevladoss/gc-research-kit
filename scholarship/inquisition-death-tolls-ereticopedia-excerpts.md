# Spanish Inquisition: number of victims — attributed excerpts

Source: Tomasz Karlikowski, "Number of victims of the Spanish Inquisition" (entry), *Ereticopedia — Dizionario di eretici, dissidenti e inquisitori nel mondo mediterraneo*, ed. Daniele Santarelli et al.

## Henningsen–Contreras database (relaciones de causas, 1540–1700)

The entry reports, for the best-documented period 1540–1700, "44.674 recorded formal trials, with 1.604 death sentences, including 826 executions in persona and 778 in effigie" (Contreras & Henningsen, "Forty-four Thousand Cases of the Spanish Inquisition (1540–1700): Analysis of a Historical Data Bank", in G. Henningsen & J. Tedeschi, eds., *The Inquisition in Early Modern Europe*, 1986).

## Range of modern scholarly estimates cited in the entry

- Ricardo García Cárcel: 120,000–150,000 trials total, execution rate ~3.5% (≈4,200–5,250 executions).
- Jean-Pierre Dedieu: ~200,000 trials, "no more than 10.000 executions".
- Joseph Pérez: ~125,000 trials, fewer than 10,000 executions.
- Low-end estimates the entry argues against: William Monter ≈2,750; Henry Kamen ≈3,000 executions.

## Entry author's own synthesis (1480–1820)

Karlikowski's tabulation arrives at roughly 7,300 executions in persona overall:

- 1480–1539: ≈5,500 executions (≈12.2% of ≈45,000 trials) — the early, most lethal phase against conversos.
- 1540–1699: ≈1,600 executions (≈1.8% of ≈90,000 trials).
- 1700–1820: ≈200 executions (≈0.8% of ≈25,000 trials).

The entry frames these figures as refuting both the inflated popular death tolls (hundreds of thousands to millions) and the lowest revisionist counts.

## Relevance to GCHAR

Modern archival scholarship (Henningsen–Contreras database and successors) puts Spanish Inquisition executions in the low thousands over ~340 years. Nineteenth-century Protestant historiography (e.g. Llorente's extrapolations, which fed authors White used) gave far higher figures (Llorente: ≈31,912 relaxed in person). Use Kamen (*The Spanish Inquisition: A Historical Revision*, 4th ed. 2014) and Peters (*Inquisition*, 1988) via reviews/abstracts at verification time; the books themselves are copyrighted and were not downloaded.
